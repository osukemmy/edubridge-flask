EduBridge — SDG 4: Quality Education

Interactive quizzes • Curated resources • Community learning

Hackathon: Vibe Coding 3.0 • August 27 – September 2, 2025

← / → to navigate • H for help

Problem

  • Students lack engaging tools to assess learning and get targeted resources.
  • Learning gaps persist in underserved schools (device + connectivity constraints).
  • Teachers spend time creating quizzes and finding quality materials.

Solution: EduBridge

Adaptive Quizzes

Lightweight quizzes with instant feedback, aligned to local curricula.

Curated Resources

Offline-friendly PDFs/videos by topic and difficulty.

Community

Students & teachers share tips, explainers, and mini-guides.

Product Snapshot

  • Web app (Flask) with clean UI: Home • Quiz • Resources • Community
  • MariaDB (or SQLite) for storing content and posts.
  • Designed for low bandwidth, mobile-first.
Open Live Demo

Impact (SDG 4)

  • Improves learning outcomes with immediate feedback.
  • Reduces teacher prep time via reusable content.
  • Inclusive access (offline-first assets, minimal data usage).

Who Benefits

Students

Practice quizzes, track progress, find bite-size explainers.

Teachers

Share quizzes/resources, view common gaps to tailor lessons.

Schools/NGOs

Low-cost tool to supplement classrooms & after-school programs.

Market & Opportunity

  • K–12 edtech in Sub-Saharan Africa growing rapidly (mobile-first).
  • Initial wedge: pilot with a cluster of schools; expand via NGO partnerships.

How It Works (Architecture)

  • Frontend: HTML/CSS (lightweight).
  • Backend: Flask routes for quiz, content, community.
  • DB: MariaDB (cloud) or SQLite fallback.
  • Deployment: Render (gunicorn).

Roadmap

  • Now: Quizzes + curated resources + community posts.
  • Next: Teacher dashboard, per-topic mastery, badges.
  • Later: Adaptive engine, SMS/USSD access, multilingual content.

Traction (Hackathon)

  • Working prototype (live).
  • Initial content sets and quizzes.
  • Deployment + GitHub repo + deck link.
